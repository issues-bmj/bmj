continued from A Second Chance


   According to Bertram Lubin, M.D., a pediatric hematologist and President of Children’s Hospital Oakland Research Institute, the most intriguing thing about cord blood is that “one can use it without a complete HLA match without complications, whereas bone marrow transplants need a complete match for best results. And because newborns are not immunologically mature, they can accept and thrive after transplantation.” Studies suggest that cord blood-derived stem cells demonstrate an increased growth and grafting potential due to their high frequency of progenitor cells per unit. Thus, placental/ umbilical cord blood seems to be a sensible alternative for both childhood and adult leukemia.

    The major limit to cord blood transplants, however, is that only a small amount of blood can be obtained from them. In addition, there is a much longer post-transplant recovery time. Like with bone marrow transplants, the patient is subjected to chemotherapy to kill the leukemic cells so that he lacks a functioning immune system during the transplant. Adults who require a higher nucleated cell dose of umbilical cord blood graft have a lower survival rate because there is a longer proliferation time. For this reason, and the fact that children are easier to treat in general, placental/umbilical cord blood transplantation has had much better results in younger patients.

    However, studies show that one can use multiple sources of cord blood in adults and the transplant can still succeed. This attests to cord blood’s versatility and viability. While transplantation success has been limited to that of childhood leukemia, adult placental/ umbilical cord blood transplantation is gaining favor as well.

    For those in the most advanced stages of these acute cancers, waiting is not a choice. While it is best to find a perfect match, this is often an unfeasible solution. As such, it is necessary to employ methods that guarantee the greatest transplant survival rate given the limited time frame, and cord blood transfusions seem to be the best alternative.
