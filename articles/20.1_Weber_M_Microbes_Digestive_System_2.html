continued from Microbes in the Digestive System


   Many bacteria and microbes are present in the body due to a person’s diet history. Because people eat a multitude of different food in varying quantities throughout their lives, the composition of bacteria is unique for every individual. However, due to the conclusions of the zebrafish experiment, scientists and doctors are learning what foods have the ability to positively or negatively affect the bacterial composition in one’s body. Foods high in fat, while always known to be less healthy than those with low fat content, are conducive to the development of Firmicutes bacteria in the digestive system. As Firmicutes molecules thrive in environments supplied with high nourishment, this serves to increase the amount of fat taken in by the body. While this can seem like an unbreakable cycle, modifications to diet can help reduce the Firmicutes population, which in turn reduces the amount of fat collected in the digestion process.

    The particular way in which Firmicutes stimulate fat absorption is still unknown. The fact that scientist even know about microbial composition, though, provides a step in a positive direction for those interested in ways to fight the obesity that has become prevalent in today’s society. The potential this discovery has to offer is immense; if more research shows scientists how these microbes stimulate fat absorption, it could open doors for medication that could be used to manage obesity and other weight-related conditions. Not only would this have the ability to help those with obesity, but it could also be applied to the opposite end of the spectrum and assist those who are malnourished gain needed weight. Though the science has not yet reached this point, the possibility that microscopic bacteria which live inside each and every person could have such a massive impact on the lives of others is a tremendous reason to further research in this field.
