continued from Marijuana As Muse


    So, is marijuana a muse that can elevate us to reach creative greatness? Were Marley, Dali, the Beatles, Shakespeare and so many other creative minds wise to pursue relationships with the drug? It is worth noting that none of the aforementioned ways that the frontal lobe or dopamine is related to human creativity alter our individual creative potentials. In fact, certain studies have even shown that marijuana may have effects that are opposed to characteristics oftentimes associated with creativity. In the report by O’Leary referenced above, cerebral blood flow increased in areas of the brain related to focused attention and, consequently, creative drive, but CBF showed reduced amounts in parts of the brain responsible for visual and auditory acuity. And somewhat more concerning are studies which reveal cannabinoid inhibition of neuronal communication. Cannabinoids can prevent the brain from making cyclic adenosine monophsophate (cAMP), a compound important in generating nerve impulses in the brain. Alongside this, research done by Mitch Earleywine in 2002 demonstrated that marijuana also decreases the rate of firing among neurons by inhibiting the release of neurotransmitters through the closing of calcium channels responsible for their release. According to Earlywine, author of Understanding Marijuana, “...by inhibiting cAMP, slowing the nerve’s firing rate, and decreasing neurotransmitter release, cannabinoids alter the communication between nerve cells.” If coordinated communication among neurons is crucial to creative production, then it seems that marijuana may also serve a detrimental role within the creative process.


    In his book, Heilman states: “…studies of creativity under normal states versus intoxicated states do not reveal that drugs enhance the production of creative works.” Nevertheless, on the physiological level, creative stimulation does occur; and our minds experience sensations that incite the craving for novelty that all creative minds know well. Marijuana’s effect on creativity may therefore reside not in its power to give birth to something new in the human mind, but to psychologically awaken, encourage and motivate what is already there.
